The Structural Question

Lilly's brand sells in three motions under one IG handle:

  1. Witchcraft store - spell jars + crystals + custom castings + decks + altar items. Physical + digital products.
  2. Author + Academy - the book and the Substack school. Recurring touch + lead artefact.
  3. 1:1 + Mentoring - £222 Clarity Session and a gated Mentoring door with no public price.

What's missing is the productised middle tier between £22-£80 store products and the implicit-high-ticket Mentoring door. The Academy is positioned as a school but isn't structured as one - it's a feed-style newsletter, not a syllabus with cohort cadence. The £222 Clarity Session is a one-off, not a structured eight-week room. So the practitioner who's read the book, bought a spell jar, joined the Academy, and wants the structured next room has nowhere to land.

The wedge sits at exactly this seam. A productised cohort, sized between the £222 Clarity Session and the gated Mentoring door, that turns the Academy's "school" positioning into an actual structured intake.

Pitch Leverage

  1. Academy positioned as a school but not productised as one. The Substack describes itself as "everything you need to know about spell casting and manifesting your team life through witchcraft." The cohort makes that explicit - a structured eight-week intake with weekly group call + scheduled office hours, three pillars, capped seats. The Substack stays as the ongoing room between cohorts.

  2. 1.2M IG + 600+ Academy + 149 verified reviews = the cohort doesn't need a cold launch. Warm-list pull from existing Academy subscribers + book buyers fills the first cohort before sales page opens to cold IG traffic.

  3. The book is the trojan horse. Manifest Like A Witch is the highest-trust artefact in the funnel. Customers who finish a 200-page book on spellwork and candle magic have already qualified themselves for the cohort. A back-matter QR + segmented book-buyer email list turns the book into a warm-list engine.

  4. Bio link is aggregator-shaped, not funnel-shaped. 1.2M followers tap the bio link and land on planoly.store, where Patreon, Amazon, podcast, and the store all compete equally for attention. The highest-intent fraction - the practitioner ready to spend £500-£1,500 on a structured room - leaves without ever seeing one. A two-door hub replaces the aggregator with a funnel.

  5. The "Which Witch Are You?" archetype quiz fits her register perfectly. Tier 1-4 readiness segmentation + Moon Witch / Hearth Witch / Sea Witch / Garden Witch archetype overlay. Captures email, routes the warm middle into the cohort waitlist, shareable enough that it travels through the audience organically.
