Bottleneck 01 · Highest Revenue Lever

The Academy is positioned as a school but isn't productised as one. The ladder runs free content to £22-£80 store products to £222 Clarity Session to gated Mentoring with nothing structured in between.

The gap. A practitioner who's read Manifest Like A Witch, bought a spell jar, subscribed to the Substack academy, and started practising on her own has no productised next room. The £222 Clarity Session is a one-hour 1:1 surface, not a structured ladder. The Mentoring door is gated behind enquiry with no public pricing - meaning the practitioner who's ready to spend £500-£1,500 for a structured eight-week room doesn't see one. She either stays at the spell-jar tier indefinitely or self-disqualifies from the Mentoring door because the price is unsurfaced.

The leak. The Academy is described in Lilly's own words as "a safe and non-judgmental space where you can start your witchcraft journey at your own pace" and "everything you need to know about spell casting and manifesting your team life through witchcraft" - a school positioning. But the school doesn't have a structured intake. It's a feed, not a syllabus. Warm Academy subscribers who want the structured version migrate to other witchcraft teachers' courses, drift, or stall. Each season without a productised cohort is a cohort cycle's worth of warm-list spend going elsewhere or going nowhere.

The fix. Manifest Like A Witch Cohort. Productised, eight-week, ~£555-£1,500 seat (final pricing tested against the warm-list quote response). Borrows the book title as the brand name and creates a structured intake of what the Academy already teaches in feed form: weekly Lilly-led group call, scheduled office hours, three pillars - Intention + Ritual Foundations / Spellwork In Practice / Candle Magic + The Moon Cycle. Capped at 60 per cohort. Two cohorts per year aligned to seasonal practice (autumn equinox intake + spring equinox intake). Cohort grads route into 1:1 Mentoring as the structured next step. Academy stays as the ongoing room between cohorts.

Cost of waiting. Roughly £60K-£120K per cohort cycle not collected. Two cycles per year on the seasonal cadence = £120K-£240K in cohort revenue lost each year the room doesn't exist. Plus the warm-list attrition - Academy subscribers who don't get a structured next door tend to renew the newsletter once or twice and drift. Six months from now, two full cohort cycles have come and gone. The competitor witchcraft teachers (most of whom do run cohort-shaped offers) keep filling against your warm audience because they opened a door you haven't.

Year-1 floor. Two cohorts per year, 60 seats per cohort, 35% fill rate on the lower end (21 seats / cohort, 42 / yr) at £555 avg = £23K Year-1 floor lower bound. Tighter conversion against the 1.2M IG + Academy + book buyer warm list at 60% fill (72 seats / cohort, 144 / yr) at £777 avg = £112K mid-range. At 80% fill (96 seats / yr if we cap holds, banded against the 1.2M tier) at £1,000 avg seat = £160K-£240K upper-conservative. The conservative band lands at £120K-£240K Year-1 floor with room above.

Bottleneck 02 · Foundational Capture Layer

The 1.2M IG bio links straight to a planoly.store aggregator with no segmentation event. Warm followers leave the funnel without ever being asked which door they're here for.

The gap. Every IG follower who taps the bio link lands on planoly.store/mysticprimrose - an aggregator with Patreon, Amazon storefront, mysticprimrose.com, podcast, and social all weighted equally. There's no quiz, no scorecard, no segmentation event. A follower who's a "ready for a structured cohort" practitioner sits in the same un-routed bucket as a follower who's a "casual viewer, not buying anything yet." The two segments need different routing. They get the same aggregator menu.

The leak. Industry standard for a scorecard quiz against a warm IG audience the size of @mysticprimrose's is a 3-5% pull rate inside 90 days. Against a 1.2M-follower base, even on the conservative end - 3% of the bio-tap fraction over 90 days - that's tens of thousands of incremental email subs segmented by stage and intent. Right now the email list is whatever subscribes to the Substack academy. Everyone else who taps the bio either buys a £22 spell jar or scrolls back. No tracked tier, no nurture, no second touch.

The fix. "Which Witch Are You?" archetype scorecard quiz. Ten questions, two-minute completion. Returns a stage read - Tier 1 (witch-curious, no practice yet), Tier 2 (solo practitioner, no structure), Tier 3 (ready for cohort, wants a room), Tier 4 (advanced, ready for 1:1 Mentoring). Plus a soft archetype overlay (Moon Witch, Hearth Witch, Sea Witch, Garden Witch) for shareability and brand fit. Captures email + segments by readiness. Tier 2 and Tier 3 route into the cohort waitlist. Tier 4 routes into Mentoring enquiry. Tier 1 stays in the Academy nurture for 60 days then re-quizzes. Quiz lives at mysticprimrose.com/quiz and replaces planoly.store in the IG bio.

Cost of waiting. Roughly £4K-£9K / month in nurture-driven cohort + Mentoring + store revenue not happening from un-segmented warm traffic. Compounds to £50K-£110K / yr in incremental revenue from a list you don't yet have. Plus the segmentation data itself is load-bearing for everything downstream - paid traffic, content prioritisation, cohort intake forecasting. Without it, every reel feeds an aggregator, not a funnel.

Year-1 floor. Conservative 3% pull rate against the 1.2M-follower base = tens of thousands of incremental email subs over 90 days. Cohort waitlist conversion off the segmented list at 1.2% + Mentoring enquiries at 0.4% + £22-£80 store conversion at 6% = £50K-£110K Year-1 floor, compounds for life of the list.

Bottleneck 03 · Two-Door Hub

The bio link is an aggregator. Aggregators distribute clicks across many low-intent surfaces. A hub directs clicks into a primary funnel and keeps the aggregator surfaces accessible for the lower-intent traffic that needs them.

The gap. planoly.store/mysticprimrose treats Patreon, podcast, Amazon storefront, mysticprimrose.com, and social all as equal-weight options. That works for an audience that mostly consumes free content. It's wrong for a brand that has £22-£950 paid offers, an Academy subscription, an author book, a £222 Clarity Session, and gated Mentoring. The aggregator hides the funnel under the noise.

The leak. Every IG follower who taps the bio gets a menu, not a doorway. The highest-intent fraction (the practitioner ready to spend £500+ on a structured room) picks the aggregator option that looks most likely to lead them somewhere - usually the mysticprimrose.com link - and lands on a store homepage that isn't built to convert that intent. She browses spell jars, reads a book description, and leaves. The intent-to-purchase journey takes too many clicks.

The fix. Two-door hub at mysticprimrose.com/with-lilly (or the bio-link slot directly). Two primary doors above the fold:

  1. For the practitioner who wants a room to do the work in → Cohort waitlist + quiz
  2. For the practitioner ready for 1:1 work → Mentoring enquiry form

One secondary surface row below: the store, the book, the Academy, the podcast, the Patreon, the Amazon storefront - all linked, all visible, all secondary to the two primary doors. The aggregator surfaces stay accessible for the audience that needs them. The funnel-shaped traffic gets routed first.

Cost of waiting. Roughly £3K-£6K / month in cohort + 1:1 enquiries not happening from highest-intent IG bio taps. Compounds to £35K-£75K / yr in revenue left in the aggregator. Plus the framing shift: an aggregator-shaped bio link signals creator brand. A hub-shaped bio link signals operator brand. The framing recruits the higher-intent half of the audience without losing the lower-intent half.

Year-1 floor. Hub conversion against existing bio-tap traffic at 1.8% to either cohort waitlist or Mentoring enquiry, weighted average ticket £500 across both = £35K-£75K Year-1 floor, before any new audience growth.

Bottleneck 04 · Book → Cohort → Academy Wiring (Closed Loop)

Manifest Like A Witch is the highest-trust artefact your audience owns. The Academy is the recurring touchpoint. Neither currently routes to the cohort because the cohort doesn't exist yet, and the wiring isn't pre-built for when it does.

The gap. The book is a static product. Customers buy it, read it, finish it, and the touchpoint ends. There's no in-book CTA, no QR code, no book-buyer email list segmentation, no "if you finished the book and want to do the practice in a room" callout. The Academy newsletter ships regularly but doesn't currently route subscribers into a structured intake (because there isn't one to route into yet). When the cohort ships, the existing book + Academy assets need to be the warm-list engine, not a sidecar.

The leak. Manifest Like A Witch is the strongest qualifying signal in the entire funnel. A reader who finishes a 200-page book on candle magic and spellwork has demonstrated more buying intent than 99% of an IG audience. Today that reader has nowhere structured to go next. She might buy a spell jar. She might join the Academy. She almost certainly won't enquire about gated Mentoring without a productised middle. The book is a half-built funnel - it converts to spell-jar purchases and Academy subscriptions, but not to high-LTV cohort enrolment.

The fix. Three small wiring moves, shipped alongside cohort 01:

  1. Book → cohort waitlist. Insert a one-page back-matter QR code in the book pointing to mysticprimrose.com/cohort-waitlist. Reprints onwards carry it natively. Existing readers get the same call-out via an Academy newsletter dedicated to book buyers.
  2. Academy → cohort intake. One Academy newsletter per quarter dedicated to "the next cohort opens [date]" - structured, time-bounded, with a story-led explanation of who the room is for. Plus the cohort waitlist signup permanent in the Academy welcome email.
  3. Cohort grads → Academy continuity. Cohort grads get a permanent Academy comp + a private cohort-alumni channel inside the Academy. The cohort feeds the Academy long-tail retention. The Academy feeds the cohort intake. Closed loop.

Cost of waiting. This one compounds with cohort 01. Without the wiring, cohort 01 launches cold against the IG. With the wiring, it launches warm against the book + Academy. The difference is roughly a 2x-3x fill rate on cohort 01 - the gap between "first cohort is a slow burn" and "first cohort fills the waitlist before the sales page goes live."

Year-1 floor. The wiring moves cohort 01's fill rate from the 35% low-bound to the 60% mid-range, plus segmented book-buyer revenue lift. Banded at £40K-£85K Year-1 floor isolated, with significant compound effect on Bottlenecks 01 + 02 above.

Bottleneck 05 · Optional / Audit Layer

The bio link is the only IG-side conversion event, and the YouTube channel descriptions don't carry quiz / cohort CTAs.

The gap. YouTube long-form is the second-strongest organic surface in the funnel after IG. Recent flagship video (May 7, 2026) is a real piece of teaching that demonstrates Lilly's voice and authority - exactly the kind of content that should route viewers to the cohort waitlist or quiz. Right now the descriptions point generically at the store or the Academy. No quiz CTA, no cohort waitlist, no segmented email capture from the YouTube traffic.

The leak. Smaller than Bottlenecks 01-04 because YouTube is a secondary surface, but real. Each YouTube view that ends without a tracked email capture is a warm-audience compound that gets reset to zero. The fix is a description-template rewrite plus pinned-comment CTA on the next three uploads.

The fix. Quiz CTA in every YouTube description + pinned comment on every video. Optional Day-30+ ship: a short cohort-intro video filmed by Lilly that lives as a permanent pinned card on the channel and links to the cohort waitlist.

Year-1 floor. Difficult to isolate from Bottlenecks 02 + 03 (these layers compound). Listed here for completeness because the wiring is the work that makes the warm-list engine self-feed across all major surfaces.

What This Plan Does NOT Touch

  • The witchcraft store SKUs. Spell jars, books, custom castings, tarot readings, crystals, altar items - all stay live, all stay priced as they are, all stay shipping.
  • The Mysticprimrose Academy newsletter cadence. Substack subscription continues. Cohort intake newsletters are added to the calendar, not replacing what's there.
  • Manifest Like A Witch. The book stays the book. The cohort uses the same brand name as the structural extension.
  • The 1:1 Clarity Session (£222). Stays as the lower-tier 1:1 entry. Cohort sits above it on the ladder.
  • The gated Mentoring door. Stays as the upper-tier 1:1 destination. Cohort grads route into Mentoring as the structured next step.
  • The IG content cadence. Reels, posts, Stories continue. CTAs re-route to the quiz / cohort waitlist / hub.
  • The Patreon, the podcast, the Amazon storefront. All stay live, all stay accessible from the secondary surface row on the hub.
