VSL Outline (Lilly films this, in her own words)

Hook (0:00-0:30) - Why I'm making this

"I've been running the Academy for a while now - the School for Witches, the Substack, all of it. And the question I get more than anything else is: what's the next step? I've read the book. I'm practising. I've bought a spell jar. I want to go deeper. And for a long time I didn't have a proper answer for that."

Direction for Lilly: Open wherever you feel comfortable. The hook is the moment you decided there needed to be a room, not just a newsletter.

The Problem (0:30-1:30) - What was missing

"The Academy is a space where you can learn at your own pace. That's the point of it - non-judgmental, self-directed, your own timeline. But I kept meeting practitioners who were ready for something more structured. A room where we're all doing the work together, on a shared cadence, with me leading the calls. And I realised the Academy doesn't do that - it's a feed, not a circle."

Direction: Name the gap in your own words. The Academy is not the cohort. The Clarity Session is not the cohort. This is the room that didn't exist until now.

The Cohort (1:30-3:00) - What this is

"So this is what the cohort is. Eight weeks. Sixty seats - I cap it because the room only works if it's actually a room. Two intakes a year, aligned to the seasonal calendar the practice already follows. A weekly group call with me. Scheduled office hours in between. Three areas of work: intention and ritual foundations, spellwork in practice, and candle magic and the moon cycle."

Direction: Walk through the structure in your own voice. The three pillars can be named in whatever order feels natural. The cap and the seasonal timing are load-bearing - say them clearly.

Who It's For (3:00-4:00) - The practitioner this is built for

"This is for the witch who's past the curious stage. Who's been practising - maybe solo, maybe just with the Academy, maybe with the book as the guide. Who wants to do the work in a room of other practitioners who are doing the same thing. It's not a beginner's space. It's not for someone who's never picked up a spell jar. It's for the practitioner who already knows the practice is real and wants structure around it."

Direction: Describe your ideal cohort member. You'll know her better than any script does. Trust your own read on who she is.

The Room (4:00-5:00) - What it actually feels like

"The calls are practitioner-led. I bring the structure - the pillar we're working on that week, the ritual, the candle magic sequence - and the room brings the practice. Office hours are for the questions that come up during the week between calls. It's not a course. You're not watching videos and checking boxes. You're doing the work alongside other witches who are doing it too."

Direction: Paint the room. What does the weekly call feel like? What does office hours look like? The audience needs to see themselves in it, not just understand the logistics.

Lilly's Credentials (5:00-5:45) - Why this from you

"I've been building this practice and this brand since I was young. From 4,000 followers to 1.2 million. Written the book. Built the Academy. Run the store. 149 verified reviews on the store at 4.84 out of 5. The cohort is the structured room version of everything the Academy teaches in feed form. It's the work, done together."

Direction: Light touch on credentials. The audience already knows you. This section is a brief grounding, not a pitch moment.

The CTA (5:45-6:30) - How to apply

"Applications are open via a short form on the page. It's not a long process - just enough for me to confirm the cohort is the right fit for where you are in the practice. Seats are capped at sixty. The intake runs on the seasonal calendar. If this is the room you've been looking for, the application is below."

Direction: Keep the CTA low-pressure. "Is the room the right fit for where you are" is the qualifying language. It's not "buy now." It's "is this yours?"
