Foundation

Copy Strategy · Lilly Statham (@mysticprimrose)

The voice, framing, and headline architecture AG1 will write the cohort, quiz, and hub copy to. Designed to sit inside the Mysticprimrose register - witchcraft, intention, ritual, candle magic, the craft, the practice - without slipping into the occult-cliche vocabulary that the lower-tier of the niche overuses.

Voice Pillars

1. Witchcraft register, soft-feminine delivery. Use the practitioner vocabulary - intention, ritual, casting, spellwork, the work, the craft, the practice, the moon cycle, candle magic, the witch's year - because that's the brand. Pair with warm, practitioner-grounded cadence (not operator-direct), because Lilly's IG voice is closer to "safe and non-judgmental space" than "founder cohort". The cohort copy speaks practitioner-to-practitioner, not strategist-to-buyer.

2. Faith-first, structure-second. Lilly leads with the practice, then the room around it. The voice respects that. The work itself (intention, ritual, candle magic, spell jars, moon cycle attunement) is the first layer the copy describes. The cohort structure (cap, cadence, office hours) is the second layer, sitting underneath the work to make it scalable. We never lead with revenue math on a sales surface. The math is in the email, the FAQ, and the hub - never in the hero.

3. Quiet authority, no occult hype. Lilly is an author, runs a 600+ member academy, has 149 verified 4.84/5 store reviews, grew from 4,000 to 1.2M followers, and built the brand from documenting her own practice on Instagram. The authority is already in the room. The copy doesn't reach for hype, doesn't reach for "secret rituals" or "ancient wisdom" framing. The voice reads as if it were already inside the Mysticprimrose world, naming the next door for the practitioner who's been here a while.

4. Outcome-grounded, never outcome-guaranteed. Witchcraft + manifestation + spiritual practice marketing sits in the same compliance bracket as coaching and mindset work. The copy describes what the cohort teaches, what the work is, and what graduates have historically practised. Never what the cohort promises in terms of specific outcomes. The "Outcomes vary by individual practice and ritual" footer line sits on every sales surface.

5. The craft is a room, not a curriculum. The cohort is a room of practitioners doing the work alongside each other and with Lilly. The voice describes the room - the calls, the office hours, the moon-cycle cadence, the shared posture - rather than a lesson plan or a syllabus. The mentorship sells as a place to practise, not as a list of modules to consume.

6. UK rhythm, global welcome. Lilly is UK-based and her audience is global with strong UK + US + AU split. The copy uses British English spelling (practise as verb, practice as noun, colour, learnt, recognise) and prices primarily in GBP. International applicants get USD parity in parentheses on the cohort sales page only. The timezone of the live calls reads explicitly so US + AU applicants self-route.

Voice Rules (DO + DON'T)

DO

  • Use Mysticprimrose vocabulary: intention, ritual, spellwork, the craft, the practice, the work, casting, candle magic, the moon cycle, the witch's year, manifestation, attunement, alignment, the practitioner.
  • Use "the room" or "the circle" instead of "the course" or "the program". The cohort is a circle of practitioners, not a curriculum.
  • Use "intake," "cohort," "seat," "office hours," "application," "cap" - operator-grade specifics that calibrate the offer against the existing 1:1 / store / Mentoring ladder.
  • Reference cohort cadence by seasonal markers (autumn equinox, spring equinox, the moon cycle, Beltane, Samhain, the witch's year) where it lands naturally. The audience reads those markers as calendar, not decoration.
  • Talk about what graduates practised in the cohort. What rituals they cast. What spell jars they built. What the moon cycle work taught them. Stay clear of promises about what they received in tangible terms.
  • Soft-land every outcome claim with practice language. "Most cohort grads name the candle-magic week as the unlock" reads correctly. "The cohort will change your life" does not.
  • Reference Lilly's authentic credentials: author of Manifest Like A Witch, 4.84/5 across 149 verified store reviews, 600+ Academy members, grown from 4K to 1.2M followers, "witchcraft on a budget" positioning.
  • Use British English spelling. Practise as a verb. Practice as a noun. Colour. Learnt. Recognise.
  • Mirror Lilly's actual cadence: "safe and non-judgmental space", "at your own pace", "I wanna help", short warm sentences, exclamation marks sparingly.

DON'T

  • Occult cliche register. No "dark arts," "spells unlocked," "secret rituals," "ancient wisdom unlocked," "manifest your destiny."
  • Spiritual-niche AI-slop. No "transform your life," "abundance secrets," "manifest the life of your dreams."
  • Hype verbs. No "revolutionary," "game-changer," "next level."
  • Guaranteed-outcome framing. No "you will manifest the job, the relationship, the six figures." Replace with method-grounded language plus the disclaimer footer.
  • Em dashes (U+2014). Use commas, hyphens, or two sentences.
  • Minute-quantified CTAs ("3-minute call," "5-minute install"). Banned in outreach surfaces per build rules.
  • Generic motivational filler. If a sentence works without the word, the word is filler.
  • Specific income promises tied to a timeline without immediate disclaimer and method-grounding.
  • Occult-stereotype imagery in copy: cauldrons, broomsticks, pointed hats, anything that reads as Halloween rather than practice.
  • Operator-direct cadence imposed on Lilly's soft-feminine register. The cohort copy speaks practitioner-to-practitioner, not strategist-to-buyer.

Copy Review Gates Before Publish

  1. Em-dash sweep - zero em-dash characters (U+2014). Hyphens, commas, or two sentences only.
  2. Banned-phrase sweep - run the banned list above against every deliverable. Zero hits before publish.
  3. Minute-CTA sweep - "in less than 7 days" is the only quantified CTA allowed in outreach. No "30-minute call," no "5-minute setup" in DM body (quiz duration shows in the quiz copy itself, not in outreach).
  4. Outcome-claim sweep - every outcome line method-grounded plus footer disclaimer present on the sales surface.
  5. British English spelling - practise / practice / colour / learnt / recognise consistent throughout.
  6. Sign-off sweep - "Andrei (AG1)" on DM. "Andrei / AG1 Consulting Ltd" on email. "Built specifically for Lilly" on hub footer.
  7. Voice match - Lilly's IG vocabulary present (intention, ritual, the craft, the practice, the work, the moon cycle, safe space, at your own pace); operator vocabulary present (cohort, seat, intake, application, office hours, cap). Both registers active, neither absent.

When all seven gates pass, the surface ships.
