30-Day Content Calendar

Week 01 · Awareness Layer: The Practice Itself

Day 01 - Reel Angle: What actually happens when you cast a spell (no mystical claims - just the honest practitioner experience) Hook: "I've been casting spells for years. Here's what actually happens." Direction: Normalise the practice for people on the edge of being interested. Not "you'll manifest your dream life." Just: this is what the work is. CTA: Academy link in bio / quiz teaser

Day 02 - Carousel Angle: Moon cycle practice for beginners - the three things you actually need Hook: "You don't need a £200 altar. Three things, and you're practising." Direction: "Witchcraft on a budget" positioning thread. Lilly's audience indexes on accessibility. CTA: Book link in bio

Day 03 - Story sequence Angle: Day-in-the-practice (what Lilly's personal rituals look like) Hook: "What my morning practice actually looks like (real, not aesthetic)" Direction: Practitioner authenticity over aesthetics. Show the candle, the journal, the unpolished reality. CTA: None - pure warmth

Day 04 - Reel Angle: Self-sabotage in spell work - the pattern Lilly sees most often Hook: "The reason your spells aren't landing (it's not your technique)" Direction: From the Substack post topic. Voice: "I wanna help" energy. CTA: Academy link / Substack subscribe

Day 05 - YouTube (longer) Angle: Intention-setting for Taurus season / next moon cycle Hook: Full tutorial - how to write an intention that lands Direction: Demonstrates the kind of depth the cohort will go into. Seed the cohort in the description. CTA: Quiz CTA in description ("Find out which Mysticprimrose room is sized for where you are in the practice")

Week 02 · Interest Layer: The School + The Gap

Day 08 - Reel Angle: Why "school" and "newsletter" are different things (soft seed of the cohort) Hook: "The Academy is a school. But not the kind with a classroom." Direction: Plant the idea of structured learning without pitching the cohort directly. Let the audience realise the gap themselves. CTA: Academy Substack

Day 09 - Carousel Angle: The four stages of a witchcraft practice (quiz archetype seed) Hook: "Where are you in the craft? Four stages, four doors." Direction: Moon Witch / Hearth Witch / Sea Witch / Garden Witch. This is the quiz result preview - it warms the audience to the concept before the quiz goes live. CTA: "Which type is you? Take the quiz. [link in bio]"

Day 10 - Story Q&A Angle: "Ask me anything about starting a witchcraft practice" Hook: AMA sticker. Lilly answers in Stories. Direction: Surface the questions that the cohort would answer in Week 01 (intention foundations). Demonstrate the demand for the structured room. CTA: Academy link

Day 11 - Reel Angle: Budget-friendly witchcraft - the five spell ingredients you actually need Hook: "You can practise with what's already in your kitchen." Direction: Hearth Witch archetype angle. High shareability, broad audience pull. CTA: None (shareable content) / optional book link

Day 12 - YouTube Angle: Candle magic tutorial - full beginner-to-practitioner walkthrough Hook: "Everything I know about candle magic in one video." Direction: Pillar 03 of the cohort (candle magic and the moon cycle). The tutorial is what the cohort goes deeper on in a room setting. CTA: Quiz CTA in description + cohort waitlist teaser

Week 03 · Desire Layer: What the Room Could Be

Day 15 - Reel Angle: "I'm building a room for the witches who are past the curious stage" Hook: Soft cohort announcement. Not a launch. An invitation. Direction: "Practitioner-to-practitioner, not teacher-to-student." Describe the room in Lilly's own words. Low-pressure. No urgency framing. CTA: Waitlist / quiz

Day 16 - Carousel Angle: What eight weeks of structured practice actually looks like (cohort week-by-week preview) Hook: "If I built a structured witchcraft cohort, here's what Week 1 would look like." Direction: Walk through Week 01 of the cohort (intention and ritual foundations). Make it concrete. The audience should be able to see themselves in it. CTA: Waitlist

Day 17 - Story series Angle: Behind the scenes - Lilly building the cohort (authenticity play) Hook: "I'm doing something I've never done before and I want to show you." Direction: Documenting the build. Real and unpolished. Creates investment in the outcome. CTA: "Drop 🕯 if you want a seat" (emoji reply CTA for easy engagement)

Day 18 - Reel Angle: "Which type of witch are you?" (quiz launch or pre-launch) Hook: "Ten questions. Two minutes. It tells you exactly where you are in the craft." Direction: Direct quiz promotion. Lead with the archetype overlay (Moon / Hearth / Sea / Garden) - more compelling than the tier-stage read for the audience. CTA: Quiz link in bio (primary)

Day 19 - YouTube Angle: Spirit work and hauntings (from Substack topics - high-interest edge of the practice) Hook: "What actually happens in spirit work (and what to do if something shows up)" Direction: Demonstrates the depth of Lilly's knowledge and the range of the cohort's practice. Not all witchcraft content needs to be cohort-adjacent. CTA: Academy + optional quiz CTA in description

Week 04 · Action Layer: Waitlist + Apply

Day 22 - Reel Angle: Cohort waitlist is open (direct announcement) Hook: "The cohort I've been building is ready. 60 seats. Here's how to get on the waitlist." Direction: Specific. Seasonal cadence named. The three pillars named. No hype. Just the door, opened. CTA: Waitlist link in bio

Day 23 - Carousel Angle: "Is the cohort for you?" (who this is for vs who it isn't) Hook: "This is not for beginners. Here's who the room is actually built for." Direction: Pre-qualify the audience. Makes the seat feel more exclusive without manufactured scarcity. CTA: Application link

Day 24 - Story series Angle: Testimonial feature - the Mysticprimrose store reviews Hook: "What people who've worked with me say. Real names, real reviews." Direction: Feature Niharika's and Dani's reviews. Reference the 149-review aggregate. Builds trust for the cohort without cohort-specific testimonials yet. CTA: Cohort waitlist

Day 25 - Reel Angle: "The questions I get most about the cohort" (FAQ format) Hook: "You asked. Here's what the cohort actually is." Direction: Address the top objections in Lilly's voice: time commitment, whether it's for beginners, what's in the room. Natural, not polished. CTA: Application link

Day 26 - YouTube Angle: Manifestation and financial freedom - the practical ritual side (from Substack topic) Hook: "Manifesting financial freedom: the practice I actually use" Direction: Cohort Pillar 01 and Pillar 03 adjacent. High-search angle for YouTube SEO. CTA: Quiz CTA in description + cohort waitlist

Platform-Specific Notes

Instagram

  • Reels: 30-90 seconds. Hook in first 2 seconds. Text overlay on key moments.
  • Carousels: 5-10 slides. Swipe-bait on slide 01. Practitioner insight from slide 02 onward.
  • Stories: Authentic, unpolished. Polls and Q&As to surface objections and desires.
  • Bio: Quiz link (primary). Store link (secondary, in secondary row on hub).

YouTube

  • Longer format (6-20 minutes). Keyword-optimised titles. Candle magic, moon cycle, manifestation, witchcraft for beginners.
  • Quiz CTA pinned in every description. Cohort waitlist teaser in every description from Week 02 onward.
  • Thumbnails: Lilly's face + text overlay. Mystical but not cliche.

Substack (Academy)

  • Newsletter cadence continues unchanged.
  • Additions: Quarterly intake newsletter ("next cohort opens [date]"). Welcome email updated with cohort waitlist link.
  • One dedicated book-buyer send when the cohort launches.

Tone Notes

  • Warm, non-judgmental, at your own pace. "Safe space" energy is the brand.
  • Funny-bordered authenticity is welcome and encouraged. "What the actual f*ck" is in the real testimonials - the audience responds to that register.
  • Practitioner-to-practitioner means Lilly doesn't talk down to her audience. She talks alongside them.
  • No occult hype register. No "ancient wisdom," "secret rituals," "manifest your destiny."
  • British English throughout (practise as verb, practice as noun, colour, learnt).
